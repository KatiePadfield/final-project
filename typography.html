Typography

Axial

Axial typography focuses on text directed around an axis (line). For my piece I started with the pink line and then branched text off either side. I then saw I had a lot of negative space and I wanted to add in a shape to make it more visually interesting. I chose the rings as looking on the website for Kher’s exhibition the background image included a spiral which I was trying to emulate and include in the majority of the typographical systems.

Radial

Radial typography is where text in arranged in lines at a normal to an arc. I chose the keep with my motif of the rings as I felt this was more appealing to look at than a solid shape and the branched text off from there. I also wanted to include some lines following the same direction as the text as I felt this broke up the text into section making it more readable.

Dilataional

Dilatational typography, similar to radial, involves an arc, however with dilatational the text forms the arc itself. I chose to split the text in the three arcs for this as I felt it made it clearer and gave it more structure. I didn’t choose to include the rings as I have done in the previous examples because I felt the text portrayed that effect nicely on its own and would become too distracting with the added shape. Instead, I opted to add in circles to fill some negative space as they tied in nicely with the typography without taking focus away from it.

Random

Random typography has no rules to it, it is randomised and so text can be displayed in anyway. For this I chose to keep the most important details as readable as I could by orientating them in a way it was still quite clear what they said. For the shapes in the background I wanted to lower their transparency as I really like being able to see the layering and how the cross sections effected each other.

Grid

Grid typography is structed in a grid formation. For this I started with the transparent white bar at the top as I thought this simulated a header and added the pink one in as a footer as I liked the symmetry this crated. I thought for the grid adding in rectangles would be most appropriate, so I created the two boxes which gave the structure to pace my text around. I chose to keep these boxes transparent as I wanted the negative space but to then make it feel more contained. I then aligned my text to either side to create the straight edges of the grid.

Modular

Modular typography is where text is placed in containers. I knew for this I wanted to include the rings again but for this I lowered the transparency and included a background to it as this made it more of a container. For the three circles I wanted their container to overlap showing it was all connected and I liked how the transparency changed when the were place on top of each other. For the text I the placed it inside each circle and the adjusted the side to fill the shape as best as it could.
